## THE COMPANIES

### LOVO, Inc.
**What they did:** Solicited voice recordings from actors under false pretenses — promised "non-commercial internal research." Then cloned their voices and sold them commercially. Actor Paul Lehrman discovered his own AI-cloned voice narrating a podcast he never recorded.

**The human cost:** Two voice actors — Paul Lehrman and Linnea Sage — [filed a class action](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/business-news/actors-hit-ai-startup-with-class-action-lawsuit-over-voice-theft-1235900689/) seeking $5M+. They also allege LOVO cloned Scarlett Johansson, Ariana Grande, and Conan O'Brien without consent.

**What NOIZY would have done differently:** Consent token required before any capture. Never Clause enforcement. No synthesis without a valid, unexpired consent token bound to the voice. The actor's kill switch would have stopped everything immediately.

### Suno
**What they did:** Trained AI music generation models on copyrighted sound recordings at ["almost unimaginable scale"](https://www.riaa.com/record-companies-bring-landmark-cases-for-responsible-ai-againstsuno-and-udio-in-boston-and-new-york-federal-courts-respectively/) without permission. Sony, UMG, and Warner Records all sued. Independent artists like Anthony Justice and David Woulard also filed suits.

**The human cost:** Musicians' life work — their recordings, their performances, their emotional labor — became training data. No consent. No royalties. No attribution. Their art was consumed to build a product that competes against them.

**The settlement reality:** WMG settled with Suno in November 2025. Suno drops "fair use" defense, implements opt-in. But [the majors refused to commit to securing creator consent](https://completemusicupdate.com/music-and-ai-2025s-developments-that-will-shape-2026s-disputes/) — they believe they own the copyright and can unilaterally opt all music into AI deals. The artists were not asked.

**What NOIZY would have done differently:** 75/25 from the first use. Creator consent before any training. Artist controls what their work trains. Provenance chain proves lineage. The artist is not a row in a dataset — they are a sovereign estate.

### ElevenLabs
**What they did:** Voice actors [Karissa Vacker and Mark Boyett sued ElevenLabs](https://aibusiness.com/nlp/actors-sue-ai-voice-generator-for-unauthorized-use-of-their-voices) for misappropriating their voices. **The human cost:** Voice actors who spent years building a craft saw their work copied without permission, without payment, without attribution. Their voices were turned into products sold to clients who no longer need to hire them.

**The partial correction:** ElevenLabs built its music model using licensed data. Merlin (indie labels) and Kobalt (indie publishers) struck a deal with opt-in/out rights. A confidential settlement was reached in Vacker v. ElevenLabs. But the original harm was done.

**What NOIZY would have done differently:** No circumvention possible. Consent-as-Code means the consent check is structural — not a policy, not a promise, but a gate that cannot be bypassed. NOIZY PROOF stamps every output with lineage. The artist always knows.

## THE LEGAL LANDSCAPE

As of 2026:
- **No federal law** covers the use of AI to mimic someone's voice
- A **patchwork of state laws** fills the void — Tennessee's ELVIS Act, California's consent laws, New York's Digital Replica Act
- The **NO FAKES Act** has bipartisan support but hasn't passed
- The **EU AI Act Article 50** requires synthetic content to be detectable/marked
- Voice is **not copyrightable** per 9th Circuit — only the recordings are
- Over **70 AI infringement lawsuits** are pending as of 2026
